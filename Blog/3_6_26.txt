"If it doesn't serve you, it's stealing from you"

This one has been hitting hard for me lately. 
As a producer, there are always artists that realize I have songwriting and production chops, hear the terms I like to work under, and just want me to produce them.
My biggest issue is that when this happens, sometimes the artists don't realize what it takes. 
To write a good song. To record it to a high standard. To market the artist brand and music.
There's so much time and energy involved. 
Sometimes the artists are moderately aware, so they try and get as much from me as they can. 
They want me to play in their band, coordinate the production process, and even pay for recordings and marketing. Then I can get a cut of any returns. 
In an ideal world, or in the successful realm of the music industy, this does work: labels and well known independents can and do get returns on their production budgets. 
However, there is so much that needs to occur before that ever happens.
The song, production, and brand all have to be super professional. 
This takes a lot of work, and if it is not all there, then any effort in one area won't do anything. 
Everything needs to be coordinated on a large scale. 
So when artists come to me and want me to produce them, I have had too much experience throwing time, effort, and money into poorly marketed brands to where I have to refuse to work with them. 
It's not the songs. It's not the production. Those are things that I can polish well.
But if the brand isn't there, I'd just be throwing my time into the void. 
This is where I must say that the project isn't serving me, is in fact stealing from me, and I must refuse the project.
